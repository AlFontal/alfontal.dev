## Design Context

This file is the authoritative design brief for frontend work in this repository.

### Users

This site is a hybrid personal portfolio and technical publication site, optimized for hiring first.

Primary audience:
- Hiring managers and technical interviewers evaluating fit, seniority, and proof of work.

Secondary audience:
- Scientific peers and collaborators assessing research domain depth, publication record, and technical credibility.

Tertiary audience:
- Consulting prospects looking for a credible, practical operator with scientific and engineering fluency.

Job to be done:
- Help a visitor quickly understand what Alejandro does, why he is credible, and where to go next for proof: CV, projects, publications, blog, or contact.

The site should present Alejandro as a health data scientist, bioinformatics practitioner, and scientific software builder with strong evidence across research, code, and writing.

### Brand Personality

Core tone:
- Warm technical rigor

Personality traits:
- Warm
- Rigorous
- Builderly

The interface should feel confident without startup hype, human without being casual, and authored rather than templated.

Emotional goals:
- Calm confidence
- Competence
- Curiosity
- Trust

Selective personal cues are welcome, but the dominant signal must stay professional, evidence-based, and useful to evaluators.

### Aesthetic Direction

The site is scientific and code-aware, but it must not look like:
- a SaaS landing page
- a startup dashboard
- a university CMS

Preferred visual direction:
- Editorial structure with technical accents
- Strong headings and deliberate reading rhythm
- Selective mono usage for metadata, proof points, workflow cues, and labels
- Restrained markers, chips, and utility surfaces
- Proof-oriented layouts where projects, publications, notebooks, and credentials do real positioning work

Code and lab motifs should be accents, not the whole system. Mono typography, terminal-like cues, and tool metaphors should support credibility and texture without taking over every surface.

Both light and dark themes are canonical. Design decisions must make each theme feel intentional, complete, and equally publishable.

Existing ingredients such as Space Grotesk, IBM Plex Mono, warm neutrals, and code-adjacent accents are directionally correct, but they are ingredients, not fixed law.

### Design Principles

1. Credibility before flourish.
   Every page should answer role, domain, and proof quickly.

2. Evidence over claims.
   Projects, publications, notebooks, workflow details, and artifacts should substantiate positioning instead of relying on broad self-description.

3. Distinctive restraint.
   Avoid generic template repetition, but also avoid loud visual gimmicks or novelty that weakens trust.

4. Readable scientific editorialism.
   Long-form writing should feel publishable and carefully structured, not like product docs or an internal dashboard.

5. Fast, semantic, accessible by default.
   Favor static-first delivery, low JavaScript, clear hierarchy, touch-friendly targets, visible keyboard focus, and reduced-motion respect.

6. Personal, but selective.
   Keep a few human details, portrait cues, and authored voice, while ensuring the professional signal remains dominant.

### Working Rules

How to design for this repo:

- The homepage should foreground positioning, domain focus, and proof, not generic biography.
- Projects should read as capability demonstrations, not gallery filler.
- Publications should read as evidence and record, not marketing cards.
- Blog and notebook posts should feel like polished research notes with editorial clarity.
- The CV can be denser and more utilitarian than the rest of the site.
- Use code-forward elements selectively for metadata, workflow, annotations, and proof surfaces.
- Preserve a clear distinction between narrative surfaces, utility surfaces, and proof surfaces so the whole site does not collapse into one repeated card pattern.
- Treat both light and dark mode as first-class during layout, contrast, focus, and motion decisions.

### Anti-Patterns

Avoid these directions:

- Generic AI gradients and startup hero patterns
- Overuse of identical rounded cards, pills, and chips on every page
- Academic sterility with no personality or authorship
- Terminal theming applied indiscriminately across the full interface
- Theme imbalance where one mode feels polished and the other feels like a fallback
- Product-marketing language that overstates claims without showing evidence
- Visual systems that make projects, publications, and writing feel interchangeable
